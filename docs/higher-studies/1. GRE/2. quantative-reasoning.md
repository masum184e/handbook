# 1. Quantitative Comparison (QC)

**Question:**

**Quantity A:**  
\(3x + 5\)  
**Quantity B:**  
\(2x + 9\)  

\(x > 0\)

**Options:**  
A. Quantity A is greater  
B. Quantity B is greater  
C. The two quantities are equal  
D. The relationship cannot be determined

**Step-by-Step Solution:**  
We compare \(3x + 5\) and \(2x + 9\).

Subtract Quantity B from Quantity A:

$$
(3x + 5) - (2x + 9) = x - 4
$$

Now:

- If \(x > 4\), then \(x - 4 > 0 \Rightarrow\) Quantity A > Quantity B  
- If \(x = 4\), then \(x - 4 = 0 \Rightarrow\) Quantities are equal  
- If \(0 < x < 4\), then \(x - 4 < 0 \Rightarrow\) Quantity B > Quantity A

Because the relationship changes depending on \(x\),  
**Answer: D. The relationship cannot be determined**

# 2. Multiple Choice (Single Answer)

**Question:**  
If \(2x - 3 = 11\), what is the value of \(x\)?  
A. 5‚ÄÉB. 6‚ÄÉC. 7‚ÄÉD. 8‚ÄÉE. 9

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

$$
2x - 3 = 11
$$

Add 3 to both sides:

$$

$$

Divide both sides by 2:

$$
x = 7
$$

**Answer: C. 7**

# 3. Multiple Choice (One or More Answers)

**Question:**  
Which of the following numbers are divisible by 6?  
‚òê 12‚ÄÉ‚òê 18‚ÄÉ‚òê 20‚ÄÉ‚òê 24‚ÄÉ‚òê 25

**Step-by-Step Solution:**  
A number is divisible by 6 if it is divisible by **both 2 and 3**.

- **12** ‚Üí even & sum of digits \(1+2=3\) ‚Üí divisible by 6 ‚úÖ  
- **18** ‚Üí even & sum of digits \(1+8=9\) ‚Üí divisible by 6 ‚úÖ  
- **20** ‚Üí even but sum of digits \(2+0=2\) ‚Üí not divisible by 3 ‚ùå  
- **24** ‚Üí even & sum of digits \(2+4=6\) ‚Üí divisible by 6 ‚úÖ  
- **25** ‚Üí not even ‚ùå

**Correct Answers:** 12, 18, 24

# 4. Numeric Entry (Free Response)

**Question:**  
Solve for \(x\): \(5x + 2 = 3x + 10\)

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

$$
5x + 2 = 3x + 10
$$

Subtract \(3x\) from both sides and subtract 2 from both sides:

$$
5x - 3x = 10 - 2
$$
$$
2x = 8
$$
$$
x = 4
$$

**Answer:** 4  
*(You‚Äôd type ‚Äú4‚Äù into the answer box.)*

# 5. Data Interpretation Set

**Table: Average Monthly Temperatures (¬∞C)**

| Month | City A | City B |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Jan   | 10     | 14     |
| Feb   | 12     | 16     |
| Mar   | 15     | 20     |

**Question:**  
What is the average temperature of City A across the three months?

**Step-by-Step Solution:**  
Average = (Sum of all values) / (Number of values)

$$
\frac{10 + 12 + 15}{3} = \frac{37}{3} = 12.33
$$

 **Answer:** 12.33 ¬∞C
