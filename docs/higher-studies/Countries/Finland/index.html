Finland

Finland has become one of the most attractive study destinations in Europe for international students, including Bangladeshis.

Finnish universities emphasize practical learning, research, and innovation, rather than rote memorization. The country is also famous for being one of the safest and most peaceful nations in the world, making it ideal for students and parents alike.

Why Study in Finland from Bangladesh​

Studying in Finland offers academic excellence along with a high quality of life. Here’s why many Bangladeshi students choose Finland:

  • World-Class Education System
    • Finland’s education system is globally admired for quality and innovation
    • Strong focus on problem-solving, critical thinking, and research
  • Global Recognition of Finnish Degrees
    • Degrees are recognized across Europe and worldwide
    • Excellent value for international careers or higher studies
  • English-Taught Programs
    • Hundreds of Bachelor’s and Master’s programs taught fully in English
    • No need to learn Finnish before starting studies
  • Focus on Innovation & Technology
    • Strong reputation in IT, AI, engineering, clean energy, and startups
    • Home to companies like Nokia, Supercell, and Rovio
  • High Quality of Life & Safety
    • Clean environment, low crime rate, and student-friendly cities
    • Ranked among the happiest countries in the world
  • Excellent Student Support Services
    • Modern campuses, career services, and student counseling
    • Equal treatment for international students

Step-by-Step Process to Study in Finland from Bangladesh​

Step 1: Research Courses & Universities​

Finland has two main types of higher education institutions:

Universities

  • Focus on academic research and theory
  • Suitable for students interested in research and PhD paths

Universities of Applied Sciences (UAS)

  • Practical, job-oriented education
  • Strong industry connections and internships

Popular fields for Bangladeshi students:

  • Information Technology & Computer Science
  • Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil)
  • Business & Management
  • Data Science & AI
  • Environmental Science
  • Health Sciences

Language of instruction:

  • Mostly English-taught programs

Step 2: Check Eligibility Requirements​

Academic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s: SSC + HSC (minimum GPA depends on program)
  • Master’s: Relevant Bachelor’s degree

English Language Requirements

  • IELTS: 6.0 – 6.5 overall (some programs require higher)
  • TOEFL iBT: 78–92

Entrance Exams / Interviews

  • Required for some UAS Bachelor’s programs
  • May include online exams or video interviews

Work Experience

  • Required for certain professional or executive programs

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents​

Typical documents include:

  • Academic transcripts & certificates
  • Valid passport
  • Updated CV
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation (1–2)
  • English language test results

Step 4: Apply to Finnish Universities​

  • Main application portal: Studyinfo.fi
  • January Joint Application: Most Bachelor’s & Master’s programs
  • September intake: Limited programs

Application fees:

Gene- rally no application fee for Finnish universities

Step 5: Receive Offer Letter​

  • Conditional Offer: Pending final documents or results
  • Unconditional Offer: All requirements fulfilled

Students must accept the offer online within the deadline.

Step 6: Pay Tuition Fee (Initial Deposit)​

  • Non-EU students must pay tuition fees
  • Payment confirms your study place
  • Payment methods include international bank transfer

Step 7: Apply for Finland Student Residence Permit from Bangladesh​

  • Residence Permit Type: Student residence permit

Required documents:

  • Offer letter
  • Tuition fee payment receipt
  • Passport
  • Proof of funds (bank statement)
  • Health insurance

Proof of funds:

  • Around €560 per month for living expenses

Processing time:

  • Usually 1–3 months

Step 8: Accommodation & Living Arrangements​

  • Student Housing: Student housing foundations (most affordable)
  • Private Accommodation: Shared apartments or studios
  • Average monthly rent:: €250 – €600 (depending on city and housing type)

Step 9: Pre-Departure Preparation​

  • Book flight tickets early
  • Arrange health insurance
  • Exchange currency
  • Pack winter clothing and documents

Step 10: Arrival in Finland & Post-Arrival Steps​

  • Register with local authorities
  • Collect residence permit card
  • Complete university registration
  • Open a Finnish bank account

6. Pros and Cons of Studying in Finland​

Pros

  • High-quality education
  • Innovative learning environment
  • Safe and peaceful country
  • Strong technology and startup ecosystem

Cons

  • High living costs
  • Cold and long winters
  • Competitive admissions

Scholarships for Bangladeshi Students in Finland​

  • Finnish Government Scholarships
  • University-specific tuition fee waivers
  • Erasmus+ scholarships

Most scholarships are partial, but some universities offer up to 100% tuition waivers based on merit.

Part-Time Work Opportunities​

  • Allowed 30 hours per week during studies
  • Typical jobs: restaurant staff, cleaner, warehouse assistant, delivery
  • Average hourly wage: €10 – €15

Post-Study Work & Career Opportunities​

  • Graduates can apply for a job-seeking residence permit
  • Strong demand in:
    • IT & Software
    • Engineering
    • Data & AI
    • Business & Logistics
    • Healthcare

Common Mistakes to Avoid​

  • Applying after deadlines
  • Submitting incomplete documents
  • Writing a weak SOP
  • Underestimating living expenses
  • Not understanding residence permit rules
