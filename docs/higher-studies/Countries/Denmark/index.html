Denmark

Danish universities are globally recognized, focus heavily on practical learning, and maintain strong connections with industries. Most programs are taught in English, making Denmark highly attractive for students from Bangladesh.

Why Study in Denmark from Bangladesh​

Studying in Denmark offers many academic and career benefits for Bangladeshi students.

  • World-Class Education System:** Denmark is home to internationally ranked universities known for research, innovation, and academic excellence.

  • Global Recognition of Danish Degrees: Degrees from Denmark are recognized worldwide, opening doors to global job markets and further studies.

  • English-Taught Programs: Most Bachelor’s and almost all Master’s programs are offered fully in English.

  • Innovative Learning Environment

    Danish education focuses on:

    • Practical learning
    • Group projects
    • Problem-solving
    • Critical thinking

  • High Quality of Life and Safety: Denmark is one of the safest countries in the world, with a high standard of living and student-friendly environment.

  • Strong Job Market and Career Opportunities

    Graduates benefit from:

    • Strong European job market
    • Post-study work options
    • Demand for skilled professionals

Step-by-Step Process to Study in Denmark from Bangladesh​

Step 1: Research Courses & Universities​

Public vs Private Universities

  • Public Universities: Government-funded, high-quality education, higher tuition for non-EU students
  • Private Institutions: Limited number, often more career-focused

Popular Fields for Bangladeshi Students

  • Engineering
  • Information Technology
  • Computer Science & Data Science
  • Business & Management
  • Environmental Studies
  • Renewable Energy
  • Design & Architecture

Language of Instruction

  • Majority of programs are taught in English
  • Danish language is not mandatory for admission

Step 2: Check Eligibility Requirements​

Academic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s: SSC + HSC (12 years of education)
  • Master’s: Relevant Bachelor’s degree
  • Diploma Programs: SSC/HSC depending on course

English Language Requirements

Most universities require:

  • IELTS: Overall 6.0–6.5 (no band less than 5.5–6.0)
  • TOEFL iBT: 83–90 (varies by university)

Work Experience

  • Usually not required
  • Some professional Master’s programs may prefer experience

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents​

Common documents include:

  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • Valid passport
  • Updated CV (Europass format preferred)
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation (1–2)
  • IELTS or TOEFL score report
  • Portfolio (for design-related programs)

Step 4: Apply to Danish Universities​

Application Intakes & Deadlines

  • Main Intake: September (applications usually close January–March)
  • Some programs may have limited February intake

Application Portals

  • Optagelse.dk (central application portal)
  • University-specific application systems

Application Fees

  • Most Danish universities do not charge application fees

Step 5: Receive Offer Letter​

Types of Offers

  • Conditional Offer: Subject to final documents or results
  • Unconditional Offer: All requirements fulfilled

Acceptance Process

  • Accept the offer online
  • Follow university instructions carefully

Step 6: Pay Tuition Fee (Initial Deposit)​

How Much to Pay

  • Usually one semester or one year’s tuition

Payment Methods

  • International bank transfer
  • Online payment portals (if available)

Step 7: Apply for Denmark Student Visa (Residence Permit) from Bangladesh​

Visa Type

  • Residence Permit for Studies

Required Documents

  • Offer letter
  • Tuition fee payment receipt
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Passport and photos
  • Academic documents
  • SOP (study plan)

Proof of Funds

  • Approx. DKK 6,397 per month
  • Bank statement or financial guarantee

Visa Processing Time

  • Typically 2–3 months

Step 8: Accommodation & Living Arrangements​

University Housing

Limited availability Affordable and convenient

Private Accommodation

  • Shared apartments
  • Student residences

Average Rent

  • DKK 3,000–5,000 per month (shared)
  • DKK 5,000–7,000 per month (single)

Step 9: Pre-Departure Preparation​

Checklist:

  • Book flight tickets
  • Arrange health insurance
  • Currency exchange
  • Prepare winter clothing
  • Carry academic documents

Step 10: Arrival in Denmark & Post-Arrival Steps​

After arrival:

  • Apply for CPR number (civil registration)
  • Collect residence card
  • Register at university
  • Open a Danish bank account
  • Register with local municipality

Pros and Cons of Studying in Denmark​

Pros

  • High-quality education
  • Innovative teaching style
  • Strong work-life balance
  • Safe and student-friendly country
  • English-taught programs

Cons

  • High living costs
  • Competitive admissions
  • Limited part-time job availability initially

Scholarships for Bangladeshi Students in Denmark​

Available Scholarships

  • Danish Government Scholarships
  • Erasmus+
  • University-specific merit scholarships

Funding Type

  • Partial tuition waiver
  • Full tuition + living allowance (limited)

Part-Time Work Opportunities​

  • Allowed 20 hours per week
  • Full-time during holidays

Common Jobs

  • Restaurant assistant
  • Cleaner
  • Warehouse worker
  • Student helper

Average Hourly Wage

  • DKK 110 – 150 per hour

Post-Study Work & Career Opportunities​

Stay-Back Options

  • Job-seeking permit after graduation

Job Market Overview

  • IT & Software
  • Engineering
  • Renewable energy
  • Data & Analytics
  • Life Sciences

Common Mistakes to Avoid​

  • Applying after deadlines
  • Submitting incomplete documents
  • Writing a weak SOP
  • Underestimating living expenses
  • Not checking visa rules properly
