Swizerland

Why Study in Switzerland from Bangladesh​

  • Quality of Education: Swiss universities focus on research, innovation, and practical skills. Degrees are highly respected worldwide.
  • Global Recognition of Swiss Degrees: A Swiss degree can open doors in Europe, the UK, North America, and Asia.
  • Multicultural Environment: Switzerland hosts students from over 130+ countries, offering a truly international experience.
  • Career and Internship Opportunities: Strong links between universities and industries provide internships and job exposure.
  • Safe Country & High Living Standards: Switzerland consistently ranks among the safest and happiest countries in the world.

Step-by-Step Process to Study in Switzerland from Bangladesh​

1. Research Courses & Universities​

Before applying, research carefully.

Public vs Private Universities​

Public Universities

  • Lower tuition fees
  • Highly competitive
  • Mostly taught in German/French

Private Universities

  • Higher tuition fees
  • More programs taught in English
  • Flexible admission criteria

Popular Fields for Bangladeshi Students​

  • Hospitality & Tourism Management
  • Business Administration
  • Engineering
  • Information Technology (IT)
  • Finance & Banking

Language of Instruction​

  • English (mostly private universities & Master’s programs)
  • German / French (public universities)

2. Check Eligibility Requirements​

Academic Qualifications​

LevelMinimum Requirement
Bachelor’sHSC completed
Master’sBachelor’s degree
DiplomaSSC / HSC (varies by institute)

English Language Requirements​

Most universities require:

  • IELTS: 6.0 – 6.5
  • TOEFL iBT: 70 – 90

(Some universities may waive IELTS if prior education was in English.)

Work Experience​

  • Required mainly for MBA or hospitality programs (1–2 years preferred)

3. Prepare Required Documents​

Common documents include:

  • Academic transcripts & certificates
  • Valid passport
  • Updated CV
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation (1–2)
  • IELTS/TOEFL result

4. Apply to Swiss Universities​

Application Timeline​

  • Fall Intake: Apply between December – April
  • Spring Intake (limited): August – October

Application Method​

  • Mostly online applications
  • Some universities may accept email or postal submissions

Application Fees​

  • CHF 100 – CHF 250 (varies by university)

5. Receive Offer Letter​

Types of Offers​

  • Conditional Offer – based on meeting conditions (IELTS, final result)
  • Unconditional Offer – all requirements fulfilled

Acceptance Process​

  • Sign acceptance letter
  • Pay initial tuition deposit

6. Pay Tuition Fee (Initial Deposit)​

  • Usually CHF 3,000 – CHF 10,000
  • Payment methods:
    • Bank transfer
    • Online payment
    • International remittance

7. Apply for Switzerland Student Visa from Bangladesh​

Visa Type​

  • National D Visa (Long-Term Student Visa)

Required Documents​

  • Offer letter
  • Proof of tuition payment
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Academic documents
  • SOP for visa

Proof of funds​

  • Proof of Funds
  • Bank statement or blocked account
  • Approx. CHF 21,000 – 25,000 per year

Visa Processing Time​

  • 8 – 12 weeks

8. Accommodation & Living Arrangements​

Accommodation Options

  • University dormitories
  • Shared apartments
  • Private studios

Average Rent

  • CHF 500 – 1,200 per month (depending on city)

9. Pre-Departure Preparation​

Checklist:

  • Flight booking
  • Health insurance
  • Currency exchange
  • Important documents folder
  • Winter clothing

10. Arrival in Switzerland & Post-Arrival Steps​

After arrival:

  • Apply for residence permit (within 14 days)
  • Register at university
  • Open a Swiss bank account

Pros and Cons of Studying in Switzerland​

Pros​

  • World-class education
  • High employability
  • Safe & stable country
  • Multilingual exposure

Cons​

  • High living costs
  • Limited part-time work hours
  • Competitive admissions

Scholarships​

Available Options

  • Swiss Government Excellence Scholarships
  • University-specific scholarships
  • Partial tuition waivers

Most scholarships are merit-based and highly competitive.

Part-Time Work Opportunities​

  • Allowed: 15 hours/week during semester
  • Full-time during vacations

Common Part-Time Jobs

  • Restaurant assistant
  • Hotel staff
  • Retail store helper
  • University assistant
  • Average Wage: CHF 20 – 25 per hour

Post-Study Work & Career Opportunities​

  • Graduates can stay 6 months to find a job
  • Strong demand in: Hospitality, Finance, IT, Engineering

Swiss work experience adds strong value to a global career.
