Hungary

Hungarian universities follow European education standards, provide modern facilities, and offer many English-taught programs. The country is also known for its safety, friendly people, and rich cultural heritage. From historic cities like Budapest and Debrecen to vibrant student life, Hungary provides a balanced academic and personal experience.

Why Study in Hungary from Bangladesh​

Studying in Hungary offers several advantages for Bangladeshi students:

  1. Affordable Tuition Fees and Living Costs: Compared to other European countries, tuition fees and living expenses in Hungary are very reasonable.

  2. EU-Recognized Degrees: Hungarian degrees are recognized across Europe and internationally, opening doors to global career opportunities.

  3. Wide Range of English-Taught Programs: Most universities offer Bachelor’s and Master’s programs fully taught in English.

  4. Strong Academic Fields

    Hungary is especially strong in:

    • Medicine and Dentistry
    • Engineering
    • Business and Economics
    • IT and Computer Science
    • Architecture and Humanities
    • Pharmacy and Health Sciences

  5. Rich Cultural Experience: Students enjoy a mix of European culture, history, festivals, and modern city life.

  6. Student-Friendly and Safe Environment: Hungary is considered safe, with a welcoming attitude toward international students.

  7. Access to European Job Market: Graduates can explore job opportunities in Hungary and other EU countries.

Step-by-Step Process to Study in Hungary from Bangladesh​

Step 1: Research Courses & Universities​

Start by researching universities and programs that match your academic background and career goals.

Public vs Private Universities

  • Public universities: Lower tuition fees, highly reputed
  • Private universities: Higher fees, flexible admission processes

Popular Fields for Bangladeshi Students

  • Medicine and Dentistry
  • Engineering
  • Business Administration
  • IT and Computer Science
  • Architecture
  • Economics
  • Pharmacy

Language of Instruction

  • Most international programs are taught in English

Step 2: Check Eligibility Requirements​

Eligibility varies by program and university, but general requirements include:

Academic Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s: SSC + HSC
  • Master’s: Recognized Bachelor’s degree
  • Diploma/Foundation: SSC or HSC (program-specific)

English Language Requirements

  • IELTS: usually 5.5 – 6.5 overall
  • TOEFL or university internal tests may also be accepted
  • Some universities offer admission without IELTS

Entrance Exams / Interviews

  • Required for Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy, and some Engineering programs

Work Experience

  • Needed for some MBA or professional Master’s programs

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents​

Prepare all documents carefully to avoid delays:

  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • Valid passport
  • Updated CV
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation (if required)
  • English language test results
  • Passport-size photographs

Step 4: Apply to Hungarian Universities​

Application Deadlines

  • Most programs: February – June
  • Medical programs may have earlier deadlines

Application Platforms

  • University official websites
  • Study in Hungary platform

Application Fees

  • Some universities charge a small application fee

Step 5: Receive Offer Letter​

After evaluation, universities issue:

  • Conditional Offer: Subject to meeting requirements
  • Unconditional Offer: All requirements already met

Confirm acceptance by submitting required documents.

Step 6: Pay Tuition Fee (Initial Deposit)​

  • Usually one semester or part of the annual tuition
  • Payment via bank transfer or online payment
  • Required for visa application

Step 7: Apply for Hungary Student Visa / Residence Permit from Bangladesh​

Visa Type

  • D Visa / Residence Permit for Study Purpose

Required Documents

  • Offer letter
  • Tuition fee payment receipt
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Health insurance
  • Proof of funds (bank statement or sponsor documents)
  • Passport and photographs

Proof of Funds

  • Shows ability to cover tuition and living costs

Visa Processing Time

  • Usually 4–8 weeks

Step 8: Accommodation & Living Arrangements​

Accommodation Options

  • University dormitories (low cost)
  • Private apartments or shared flats

Average Rent

  • Dormitory: €100–200/month
  • Private housing: €250–450/month

Step 9: Pre-Departure Preparation​

  • Book flight tickets
  • Arrange health insurance
  • Exchange currency
  • Prepare essential documents
  • Pack according to weather and student needs

Step 10: Arrival in Hungary & Post-Arrival Steps​

After arrival:

  • Collect residence permit card
  • Register address
  • Complete university enrollment
  • Open a local bank account

Pros and Cons of Studying in Hungary​

Pros

  • Affordable education in Europe
  • High-quality medical and technical programs
  • Many English-taught courses
  • Safe and welcoming environment

Cons

  • Hungarian language barrier outside universities
  • Limited part-time jobs in smaller cities
  • Cold winters

Scholarships for Bangladeshi Students in Hungary​

Stipendium Hungaricum Scholarship

  • Fully funded
  • Covers tuition, accommodation, stipend, and insurance

Erasmus+

  • Exchange and degree mobility programs

University-Specific Scholarships

  • Merit-based or partial tuition waivers

Funding Types

  • Fully funded
  • Partially funded

Part-Time Work Opportunities​

  • Allowed up to 24 hours per week
  • Common jobs: restaurants, cafes, delivery, retail, campus jobs
  • Average hourly wage: €4 – €7

Post-Study Work & Career Opportunities​

  • Graduates can apply for job-seeking residence permits
  • Strong demand in:
    • Medicine and healthcare
    • Engineering and IT
    • Business and finance
    • Tourism and hospitality
    • Research and academics

Common Mistakes to Avoid​

  • Applying too late
  • Missing entrance exams or interviews
  • Submitting incomplete documents
  • Writing a weak SOP
  • Underestimating living expenses
  • Not understanding visa regulations
