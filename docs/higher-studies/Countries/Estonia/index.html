Estonia

Estonian universities are globally recognized, especially for programs taught in English, and the country is famous for its advanced IT, technology, and startup ecosystem.

Why Study in Estonia from Bangladesh​

Studying in Estonia offers many academic and personal advantages for Bangladeshi students.

Key Benefits of Studying in Estonia​

  • Modern and innovative education system
  • EU-recognized degrees accepted worldwide
  • Wide range of English-taught Bachelor’s and Master’s programs
  • Strong focus on IT, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Business, and Engineering
  • Affordable tuition fees compared to other EU countries
  • Safe, peaceful, and student-friendly society
  • Growing job market and startup ecosystem
  • High quality of life with advanced digital services

Estonia is especially attractive for students interested in technology-driven careers and future-focused education.

Step-by-Step Process to Study in Estonia from Bangladesh​

Step 1: Research Courses & Universities​

Start by researching Estonian universities and programs that match your academic background and career goals.

Public vs Private Universities

  • Public universities: More affordable and widely preferred
  • Private institutions: Limited in number and usually higher in cost

Popular Fields for Bangladeshi Students

  • Information Technology (IT)
  • Data Science & Artificial Intelligence
  • Cybersecurity
  • Business & Management
  • Engineering
  • Public Administration
  • Environmental Studies

Language of Instruction: Most international programs are taught fully in English.

Step 2: Check Eligibility Requirements​

Eligibility varies by program and university.

Academic Requirements

  • Bachelor’s programs: SSC + HSC (12 years of education)
  • Master’s programs: Recognized Bachelor’s degree
  • Diploma programs: HSC or equivalent

English Language Requirements

Most universities require:

  • IELTS: Overall 5.5–6.5

  • TOEFL iBT: 72–90

    (Some universities may waive IELTS if previous education was in English)

Work Experience

  • Usually not required
  • May be needed for MBA or executive programs

Step 3: Prepare Required Documents​

Prepare all documents carefully to avoid delays or rejection.

Required Documents Checklist

  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • Valid passport
  • Updated CV
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation (1–2)
  • English language test results
  • Portfolio (for design or creative programs)

Step 4: Apply to Estonian Universities​

Application Intakes

  • Main intake: Autumn (September)
  • Applications usually open from December to March

Application Portals

  • StudyinEstonia.ee
  • Individual university online portals

Application Fees

  • Many universities charge €50–€100
  • Some universities offer free applications

Step 5: Receive Offer Letter​

After evaluation, universities issue:

  • Conditional offer: Subject to meeting requirements
  • Unconditional offer: All requirements already met

You must formally accept the offer within the given deadline.

Step 6: Pay Tuition Fee (Initial Deposit)​

Most universities require payment of:

  • Full first-year tuition or
  • Partial deposit (€1,000–€3,000)

Payment Methods

  • International bank transfer
  • Online payment systems (varies by university)

Step 7: Apply for Estonia Student Visa / Residence Permit from Bangladesh​

Bangladeshi students apply for a Temporary Residence Permit for Study.

Visa Type

  • Long-stay D-Visa (initial entry) or
  • Temporary Residence Permit (preferred)

Required Documents

  • University offer letter
  • Tuition fee payment proof
  • Valid passport
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Health insurance
  • Proof of funds (bank statement or sponsor)
  • Visa application form

Proof of Funds

  • Approximately €6,000–€8,000 per year

Processing Time

  • Usually 30–60 days

Step 8: Accommodation & Living Arrangements​

Accommodation Options

  • University dormitories (most affordable)
  • Private shared apartments
  • Studio apartments

Average Monthly Rent

  • Dormitory: €150–€300
  • Shared apartment: €250–€400
  • Studio apartment: €400–€600

Step 9: Pre-Departure Preparation​

Important Pre-Departure Tasks

  • Book flight tickets early
  • Arrange health insurance
  • Exchange currency
  • Prepare documents folder

Packing Checklist

  • Warm clothing (important for winter)
  • Academic documents
  • Medicines and prescriptions
  • Laptop and adapters

Step 10: Arrival in Estonia & Post-Arrival Steps​

After arriving in Estonia, complete the following:

  • Register your residence address
  • Collect residence permit card
  • Complete university registration
  • Open a local bank account
  • Get public transport student card

Pros and Cons of Studying in Estonia​

Pros

  • Affordable tuition and living costs
  • Digital society and innovation
  • EU-recognized degrees
  • Safe and peaceful environment

Cons

  • Limited part-time jobs in smaller cities
  • Cold winters
  • Smaller country with fewer universities

Scholarships for Bangladeshi Students in Estonia​

Available Scholarships

  • Estonian Government Scholarships
  • Dora Plus / Archimedes Foundation
  • Erasmus+
  • University-specific scholarships

Scholarships may be partial or full, depending on program and funding.

Part-Time Work Opportunities​

  • Allowed to work without hourly restrictions (as long as studies are not affected)
  • Common jobs:
    • Restaurant staff
    • Delivery services
    • Retail assistant
    • IT support (for skilled students)

Average Hourly Wage: €4 – €7

Post-Study Work & Career Opportunities​

Stay-Back Options

  • Graduates can stay to search for jobs
  • Residence permit extension available

High-Demand Industries

  • IT & Software Development
  • Cybersecurity
  • Engineering
  • Business & Finance
  • Startups and tech companies

Estonia’s startup-friendly environment offers strong long-term career prospects.

Common Mistakes to Avoid​

  • Applying late
  • Submitting incomplete or incorrect documents
  • Writing a weak SOP
  • Underestimating living expenses
  • Not understanding visa rules
