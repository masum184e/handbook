Listening

Total duration: 30 minutes (plus 10 minutes extra to transfer answers to the answer sheet)
Total questions: 40
Total sections: 4 (each with 10 questions)
Marking: 1 mark per correct answer
Question types: 6–10 different question formats appear in each test

Test Structure (4 Sections)​

SectionType of RecordingTopic & ContextNumber of Questions
1Conversation between 2 people in a social contextEveryday situation (e.g., booking, inquiry, travel info)10
2Monologue in a social contextInformational talk (e.g., tour guide, event, city services)10
3Conversation among 2–4 people in an academic contextDiscussion (e.g., students talking to tutor, project discussion)10
4Monologue on an academic subjectLecture or presentation (e.g., university topic)10

Types of Questions​

You’ll face a variety of question types that test your listening for main ideas, details, opinions, and specific information.

Question TypeDescriptionExample Task
1. Form/Note/Table/Flow-chart/Diagram CompletionFill in missing information based on what you hear.Complete notes about a lecture or tour.
2. Multiple ChoiceChoose one correct answer (A, B, C) or sometimes more than one (A–C).What is the speaker’s main concern?
3. MatchingMatch information (e.g., names, opinions, or places) with options.Match researchers to their findings.
4. Map or Plan LabelingIdentify and label locations or directions.Label rooms on a floor plan.
5. Sentence CompletionFill in blanks with words from the audio (usually ≤3 words).The new library will open on _______.
6. Short Answer QuestionsAnswer brief factual questions from the recording.What two things must students bring?
7. ClassificationCategorize items based on what you hear.Classify methods as “traditional” or “modern.”

Timing & Transfer​

  • Each recording is played only once.
  • You get a few seconds before each part to read the questions.
  • After each section, you get time to check your answers.
  • At the end, you have 10 minutes to transfer answers to the answer sheet (write neatly and check spelling).

Tips for Success​

✅ Read questions before the audio starts.
✅ Watch for synonyms — the recording rarely uses the exact words from the question.
✅ Listen for signpost words (e.g., “however,” “on the other hand”).
✅ Practice spelling and numbers carefully.
✅ Don’t leave blanks — always guess if unsure.

Section 1: Conversation (Example)

Context: A student is calling a travel agency to book a bus ticket.

Audio Transcript (Simplified Sample)​

Agent: Good morning, City Travel Agency. How can I help you?
Student: Hi, I’d like to book a bus ticket from London to Bristol, please.
Agent: Certainly. When would you like to travel?
Student: This Friday, in the afternoon.
Agent: There’s one at 2:30 pm and another at 5:00 pm.
Student: I’ll take the 5:00 pm bus.
Agent: Okay. One-way or return?
Student: Return, please. Coming back on Sunday morning.
Agent: All right. Can I have your full name, please?
Student: It’s Michael Carter — that’s C-A-R-T-E-R.
Agent: Thank you. And your contact number?
Student: It’s 07865 423 119.
Agent: Great. The fare is £25 for a one-way or £45 return.
Student: Fine, I’ll pay by card.
Agent: You’ll get your ticket by email within the next two hours.
