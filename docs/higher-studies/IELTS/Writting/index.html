Writing

Duration: 60 minutes total
Tasks: 2 (Task 1 and Task 2)
Scoring: Task 2 is worth twice as many marks as Task 1
Writing style: Formal or semi-formal (academic tone)

TASK 1 – Academic Report (Visual Description)​

What you have to do:​

Write a 150-word report summarizing and comparing information from a visual input such as:

  • Graph (line/bar/pie)
  • Table
  • Chart
  • Diagram / Process / Map

You must:

  • Summarize main trends or features
  • Compare data where relevant
  • Avoid personal opinions or explanations

Common Question Types​

TypeDescriptionExample
Line GraphShows trends over time“The graph below shows the number of international students in the UK between 1990 and 2020.”
Bar ChartCompares quantities“Compare the percentage of men and women working in different sectors in 2010.”
Pie ChartShows proportions“The pie charts show how household spending changed in 2000 and 2020.”
TableDisplays data in rows/columns“The table gives information about water usage in different countries.”
Process DiagramExplains a process (natural or man-made)“The diagram shows how plastic bottles are recycled.”
MapShows geographical changes“The maps illustrate changes in a town before and after redevelopment.”
Mixed/Multiple GraphsMore than one visual“The bar chart and line graph show different aspects of the same topic.”

Structure for Task 1​

  1. Introduction – Paraphrase the question
  2. Overview – Describe the main trend(s) or key features
  3. Body Paragraph 1 – Detail and compare specific data
  4. Body Paragraph 2 – Continue with more details/comparisons

TASK 2 – Essay Writing (Argumentative / Discussion / Opinion)​

What you have to do:​

Write a 250-word essay responding to a point of view, argument, or problem.

You must:

  • Present and support your opinion logically
  • Use examples and clear paragraphing
  • Write formally (no contractions, no slang)

Common Question Types​

TypeDescriptionExample Question
Opinion (Agree/Disagree)Express how much you agree or disagree“Some people think technology makes our lives more complicated. To what extent do you agree or disagree?”
Discussion (Both Views)Discuss both sides and give your opinion“Some think parents should teach children how to be good members of society, while others believe schools should do this. Discuss both views and give your opinion.”
Problem/SolutionIdentify causes and suggest solutions“Many cities face traffic congestion. What are the causes and what solutions can be implemented?”
Advantages/DisadvantagesWeigh pros and cons“What are the advantages and disadvantages of studying abroad?”
Double Question (Direct Questions)Answer two or more questions“Why is music important to society? What types of music are popular among young people?”

Structure for Task 2​

  1. Introduction – Paraphrase question + state your thesis
  2. Body Paragraph 1 – Present first main idea with support
  3. Body Paragraph 2 – Present second idea / counterargument
  4. Conclusion – Summarize and restate your opinion

Marking Criteria (for Both Tasks)​

Each task is graded on 4 equal criteria:

CriteriaWhat it Means
Task Achievement / ResponseHave you fully answered the question and covered all parts?
Coherence and CohesionIs your writing logically organized and easy to follow?
Lexical ResourceIs your vocabulary varied and accurate?
Grammatical Range and AccuracyDo you use a mix of correct sentence structures?

Time Management Tips​

  • Task 1 → 20 minutes (about 150–170 words)
  • Task 2 → 40 minutes (about 250–280 words)
  • Always proofread for grammar and spelling in the last 3–5 minutes

IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 – Sample Question & Band 9 Answer

Question​

The charts below show the percentage of electricity produced from different energy sources in France in 1990 and 2010.
Summarize the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

Band 9 Model Answer (≈180 words)​

The pie charts compare how electricity was generated in France from five different energy sources in 1990 and 2010.

Overall, it is clear that nuclear power became the dominant source of electricity over the 20-year period, while the use of coal and oil declined significantly.

In 1990, coal was the largest contributor to electricity production, accounting for 35%, followed by nuclear power at 25%. Oil and natural gas produced similar proportions of energy (20% and 15% respectively), while renewables made up only 5%.

By 2010, nuclear energy had increased dramatically to 65%, more than doubling its previous share. In contrast, coal and oil dropped to 10% and 5% respectively. Natural gas remained stable at around 15%, whereas renewable sources saw a modest increase to 5%.

In summary, the most notable change over the two decades was the shift towards nuclear energy, which became the main provider of electricity in France, replacing coal as the leading source.

IELTS Academic Writing Task 2 – Sample Question & Band 9 Answer

Question​

Some people believe that universities should focus on providing graduates with the knowledge and skills needed in the workplace.
Others think that universities should primarily focus on academic knowledge, even if it is not useful for a job.
Discuss both views and give your opinion.

Band 9 Model Essay (≈290 words)​

The role of universities has long been debated. While some argue that higher education should mainly equip students with practical skills for employment, others believe that universities should prioritize academic knowledge. In my opinion, both purposes are essential, but practical preparation should be slightly prioritized.

Those who emphasize academic learning claim that universities are centers of intellectual development rather than job training institutes. They argue that studying subjects such as philosophy, history, or pure science cultivates critical thinking and creativity, which can indirectly benefit society. For example, academic research often leads to breakthroughs that have long-term economic and social benefits, even if they are not immediately job-oriented.

However, others argue that with rising tuition fees and a competitive job market, universities should ensure that graduates are employable. Courses that teach practical and technical skills—such as computer programming, business management, or engineering—prepare students to contribute productively to the economy. Graduates who can directly apply their skills at work are more likely to find stable employment and repay their educational investments.

In conclusion, while academic study remains vital for intellectual growth and innovation, universities should focus slightly more on equipping students with skills relevant to modern workplaces. Balancing theory and practice will allow graduates to adapt successfully to the demands of today’s job market.

Band 9 Features Highlighted​

✅ Clear, well-structured paragraphs
✅ Variety of complex and compound sentences
✅ Wide range of academic vocabulary (e.g., cultivates, breakthroughs, employable)
✅ Precise grammar and no spelling errors
✅ Balanced argument and clear opinion
