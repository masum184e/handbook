# Speaking

**Total duration:** 11–14 minutes  
**Format:** Face-to-face interview with an examiner  
**Number of parts:** 3 (Part 1, Part 2, Part 3)  
**Purpose:** To assess your spoken English — fluency, pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammar.  
**Same for:** Academic and General Training  


## Part 1 – Introduction and Interview (4–5 minutes)

### Focus:
General questions about you, your home, studies, work, hobbies, or daily life.

### Question Pattern:
- 3 main topics (each with 3–5 short questions)  
- Questions are simple and conversational.

### Common Topic Areas:
- Hometown or current city  
- Studies or work  
- Family and friends  
- Hobbies and free time  
- Food, weather, travel, reading, music, sports, etc.

### Example Questions:
- What do you like most about your hometown?  
- Do you prefer studying in the morning or evening?  
- What kind of music do you enjoy?  
- How often do you use the internet?  


## Part 2 – Long Turn / Cue Card (3–4 minutes)

### Focus
Talk continuously for **1–2 minutes** about a specific topic given on a cue card.  
You’ll get **1 minute to prepare and make notes.**

### Structure
- Examiner gives a cue card with a topic and **4 bullet points**.  
- You must cover all points in your talk.

### Example Cue Card
**Describe a book you recently read.**  
You should say:
- what the book is  
- who wrote it  
- what it is about  
- and explain why you liked or disliked it  

### Tips
- Speak for the full 2 minutes.  
- Use linking words (*firstly, actually, on the other hand*, etc.).  
- Add personal feelings and examples.  


## Part 3 – Discussion (4–5 minutes)

### Focus
A **two-way discussion** related to the Part 2 topic but at a more abstract and analytical level.

### Question Pattern
- 4–6 questions.  
- Topics are deeper: social, cultural, or global issues.  
- Examiner may ask follow-up questions.

### Example Topics and Questions
If Part 2 was about a book you read:
- How important is reading for children?  
- Do you think people read less nowadays? Why?  
- How has technology changed the way people read?  

Other examples:
- Should history be taught at school?  
- Is it better to live in a big city or a small town? Why?  


## Scoring Criteria (Each out of 9 bands)

| **Criteria** | **What it means** |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Fluency and Coherence | Speaking smoothly and logically |
| Lexical Resource | Vocabulary range and accuracy |
| Grammatical Range and Accuracy | Use of complex and correct grammar |
| Pronunciation | Clarity, intonation, and stress |

**Final score = average of these four.**


## Overall Timing Summary

| **Part** | **Task** | **Duration** |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Part 1 | Introduction & Interview | 4–5 min |
| Part 2 | Cue Card Talk | 3–4 min |
| Part 3 | Two-way Discussion | 4–5 min |
| **Total** |  | **11–14 min** |


# Sample IELTS Academic Speaking Test


## Part 1 – Introduction & Interview (4–5 minutes)

### **Topic 1: Hometown**

**Questions:**
- Where is your hometown?  
- What do you like about your hometown?  
- Has your hometown changed much in recent years?

**Model Answers:**
> I come from **Chittagong**, which is a port city in the southeast of Bangladesh.  
> I really like the natural beauty there, especially the hills and the sea. It’s a peaceful place to relax.  
> Yes, it has changed a lot. Many new buildings and shopping centers have been constructed, which has made the city more modern but also busier.

**Tips for Band 9:**
- Give detailed answers, not just “yes” or “no.”  
- Use varied vocabulary: *constructed, modern, peaceful.*  
- Speak fluently with natural pauses.


### **Topic 2: Hobbies**

**Questions:**
- What do you usually do in your free time?  
- Do you prefer indoor or outdoor activities? Why?

**Model Answers:**
> In my free time, I enjoy reading novels and listening to music. Occasionally, I also like to go cycling with friends.  
> I prefer outdoor activities because they allow me to relax and get some fresh air. For example, cycling is both enjoyable and healthy.

**Band 9 Tips:**
- Use linking words: *occasionally, for example.*  
- Add a reason or example for your preference.


## Part 2 – Cue Card / Long Turn (3–4 minutes)

**Cue Card Topic:**  
**Describe a memorable trip you went on.**  
You should say:
- Where you went  
- Who you went with  
- What you did there  
- And explain why it was memorable  

**Preparation Time:** 1 minute  
**Talking Time:** 1–2 minutes  

**Model Answer:**
> Last year, I went on a trip to **Cox’s Bazar** with my family. It’s the longest natural sea beach in the world. We stayed there for three days. During the trip, we swam in the sea, walked along the beach, and tried some local seafood, which was absolutely delicious.  
> I particularly enjoyed watching the sunrise early in the morning; it was breathtaking. This trip was memorable because I got to spend quality time with my family, and the scenery was unforgettable. I also felt relaxed and recharged after the hectic routine of work and study.

**Band 9 Tips:**
- Speak for the full 1–2 minutes.  
- Use descriptive language: *breathtaking, quality time, unforgettable.*  
- Include personal feelings.  
- Use linking words: *particularly, also, which was.*


## Part 3 – Discussion (4–5 minutes)

**Topic:** Travel & Leisure *(related to Part 2)*

### **Questions and Model Answers**

**Do you think travel is important for people?**  
> Yes, I think travel is very important because it broadens one’s perspective. By visiting new places, people can learn about different cultures, meet new people, and gain experiences that they cannot get from books or the internet.


**How has tourism changed in recent years?**  
> Tourism has changed significantly due to technology and social media. People now research destinations online and share their experiences on platforms like Instagram, which has made traveling more accessible and popular. However, it has also led to overcrowding in some tourist spots.


**Do you prefer traveling alone or with others? Why?**  
> I prefer traveling with others, especially family or friends, because it’s more enjoyable and safer. Sharing experiences and creating memories together makes the trip more meaningful.


**What are the advantages and disadvantages of tourism?**  
> The advantages include boosting the economy, creating jobs, and promoting cultural exchange.  
> On the downside, tourism can damage the environment, cause overcrowding, and sometimes lead to cultural exploitation.


**Band 9 Tips:**
- Give extended answers with reasons and examples.  
- Use complex grammar: *By visiting new places, people can learn…*  
- Use varied vocabulary: *broaden perspective, overcrowding, cultural exchange.*  
- Avoid short, one-word answers.


## General Tips for Band 9 Speaking

| **Aspect** | **What to Do** |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Fluency & Coherence** | Speak naturally, don’t hesitate too much. |
| **Vocabulary** | Use a wide range of words and idioms appropriately. |
| **Grammar** | Mix simple and complex sentences; avoid frequent mistakes. |
| **Pronunciation** | Maintain clear stress and intonation; ensure speech is understandable. |
| **Examples & Opinions** | Always justify your answers with reasons or examples. |
