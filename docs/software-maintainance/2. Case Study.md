## 1️⃣ Challenges in Software Maintenance

The project faced several major software maintenance challenges:

### 1. High Number of Defects (Corrective Burden)

* 1400 bugs found in 2 million lines of code (1999 audit).
* 200 serious bugs still remaining in 2000.
* Required over a year just to reduce defect count.
* Risk of undiscovered critical faults affecting safety.

### 2. Over-Complex Bespoke System

* Built from scratch instead of using off-the-shelf components.
* Required new hardware + new software.
* Increased integration complexity.
* Harder to test and stabilize.

### 3. Changing Ownership & Management Disruption

* Project transferred from **IBM** to another company (later taken over).
* Caused knowledge loss, delays, and coordination problems.

### 4. Technological Obsolescence Risk

* Software expected to last 25–30 years.
* Technology advances every 12–18 months.
* Risk of system becoming outdated quickly.

### 5. Safety-Critical Environment

* System failure could lead to loss of life.
* Controllers working under stress due to delays.
* Staff had to be trained while old system still overloaded.

### 6. Schedule Slippage & Cost Overruns

* 6 years late.
* £300 million over budget.
* Deadlines repeatedly missed — classic early warning sign of project instability.

### 7. Dependence on Scrapped US Template

* The US template system was scrapped in 1995.
* Increased uncertainty and technical risk.

## 2️⃣ Types of Software Maintenance Required

Software maintenance is generally classified into four types:

### 🔹 A. Corrective Maintenance

(Fixing defects after development)

#### Needed Changes:

* Debugging 1400 identified bugs.
* Fixing serious safety-critical errors.
* Repairing system instability issues.
* Addressing integration faults between hardware and software.

**In this case:**
Massive corrective maintenance effort was required before the system could go live.

### 🔹 B. Perfective Maintenance

(Improvements to enhance performance or functionality)

#### Needed Changes:

* Increasing flight-handling capacity.
* Improving controller interface usability.
* Enhancing system performance under high traffic load.
* Optimizing response time.
* Improving reporting and monitoring features.

**In this case:**
The new centre aimed to handle more flights and provide spare capacity — a perfective goal.

### 🔹 C. Adaptive Maintenance

(Changes to adapt to environment or external changes)

#### Needed Changes:

* Adapting to increased air traffic growth.
* Updating system to new aviation regulations.
* Adjusting to new hardware platforms.
* Handling new radar technologies.
* Integrating with European air traffic systems.

**In this case:**
Rapid growth in air traffic and technology change required major adaptation.

### 🔹 D. Preventive Maintenance

(Changes to prevent future problems)

#### Needed Changes:

* Code refactoring to reduce complexity.
* Improved documentation.
* Stronger testing procedures.
* Risk assessment for safety-critical modules.
* Modular design for easier upgrades.
* Regular software updates (like French system example).

**In this case:**
Better preventive maintenance could have:

* Reduced number of serious bugs.
* Prevented project delays.
* Reduced stress on controllers.
* Improved long-term sustainability.
